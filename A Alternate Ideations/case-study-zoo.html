The Challenge

The Trust Gap

Gen Z is the most environmentally aware generation in history — and the least convinced that institutions are doing anything real about it. Zoos market themselves as conservation organizations, but for a generation raised on transparency, the gap between the narrative and the receipts is hard to ignore.

This project asked: what would it take to make a zoo genuinely credible — not just legible — to the people who already know how to spot greenwashing?

Research Signals

What the data kept saying

Four signals emerged across the research — not as findings to present, but as design constraints to work inside of.

Ethical Signal

Discomfort is Data

Suppressing moral tension degrades ethical signal. Designing with Dissonance reframes discomfort as:

Signal → Containment → Context → Choice → Closure

When tension is responsibly held instead of smoothed over, trust increases.

Implication: Ethical systems require intentional friction, not just seamless UX.

Emotional Honesty

Some Truths Should Feel Sad

In ethically charged spaces, some moments should land as grief, tenderness, or quiet reflection.

When systems try to overwrite that with spectacle, visitors sense the dodge.

Implication: Honor sadness with context and space to feel. Trust grows when the system doesn't rush people past the truth.

Experience Economy

Scarcity Creates Meaning

A shift from "see everything in a day" to species-specific, modular encounters increases:

  • Depth of engagement
  • Emotional resonance
  • Revenue per capita

Implication: Intimacy scales better than spectacle.

Alignment

Revenue and Ethics Can Align

Boutique experiences — sunrise yoga, sound baths, zero-waste concessions — create higher willingness to pay, stronger emotional alignment, and transparent conservation pathways.

Implication: Conservation credibility can become a revenue advantage.

Research Framework

A Now, New, Next look

The research was structured across three horizons — where we are, what's emerging, and where this could go. Each horizon shaped what kinds of interventions were worth designing.

Now
Where we are

Zoos face a credibility crisis with younger audiences. The spectacle model that sustained them for decades is the exact thing Gen Z distrusts most.

New
What's emerging

Conservation funding data is public — 990s, IRS filings, mission statements. But it's never been made legible to the people asking the questions.

Next
Where this could go

A zoo that shows its receipts. Experiences designed around honesty rather than aspiration. Revenue models that don't require hiding the gap.
