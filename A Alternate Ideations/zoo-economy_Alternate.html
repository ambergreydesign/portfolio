The Zoo Economy

Money In - Money Out

This map visualizes the financial relationship between U.S. zoos and the conservation missions they promote. It's built from IRS Form 990 data for AZA-accredited, non-profit, non–city-owned zoos in North America, averaged across 2019, 2021, and 2023 to smooth out COVID-era fluctuations. Each zoo's data was drawn from publicly available filings on ProPublica's Nonprofit Explorer, cross-referenced with Schedule F (foreign giving) and Schedule I (domestic giving) disclosures. The result is a composite picture of how much zoos earn, how much they give, and how closely those actions align with their stated conservation values.

 

Rather than ranking institutions, the map reveals patterns, highlighting the relationship between message and action. Together, the colors, sizes, and halos invite viewers to explore how capacity, commitment, and integrity interact across the zoo landscape.

 

Through the Greenwashing Index (GWI) , this map establishes a baseline for understanding what zoos are truly communicating through their actions, not just their words. By visualizing financial and conservation data, it reveals how each institution's choices shape what the public learns, what values stick, and where gaps may unintentionally undermine conservation goals.

Paired with upcoming tools for emotional and educational literacy, the Index invites reflection and collaboration, helping zoos align mission, message, and impact through transparent, adaptive design.

 

Data source: AZA-accredited, non-profit, non-city-owned zoos in North America. All financial data were drawn from IRS Form 990s available on ProPublica.org. Figures represent three-year averages (2019, 2022,
