Healthcare · 2024

Reducing Anxiety in Patient Onboarding

18 contextual interviews revealed that patients weren't confused by the interface — they were scared. The UI was the least of our problems.

They kept asking "is this normal?" — they needed reassurance, not features.
Method Contextual Inquiry
Outcome −38% drop-off
Team 6 people
Fintech · 2023

Why Users Ignored the "Best" Feature

Eye-tracking combined with think-aloud sessions showed the feature wasn't hidden — it was positioned where users expected ads. Banner blindness, not discoverability.

Users' eyes went exactly where we'd put it. They just refused to look there.
Method Eye-tracking + TA
Outcome +210% engagement
Team 4 people
Emotional Journey
EdTech · 2023

Mapping the Learning Confidence Curve

Longitudinal diary study over 6 weeks with 24 students. Confidence wasn't linear — it crashed at week 3 for almost everyone, for reasons that had nothing to do with content.

Week 3 is when they stopped believing it was possible — not when they stopped trying.
Method Diary Study
Outcome +52% completion
Duration 6 weeks

About

I became a researcher because I was a terrible designer.

Early in my career, I kept building things people didn't use. Not because the visuals were wrong — because I'd never actually asked anyone what they needed. The humbling moment came when a user picked up my prototype and said "Oh, I thought this was a game."

That was the best thing that ever happened to my career. I stopped guessing and started listening. Now I help teams do the same.

I'm based in San Francisco, work across product, service, and systems design, and I'm especially drawn to problems where the emotional stakes are high — health, money, education, belonging.
