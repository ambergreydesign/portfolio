Plug Redesign

Have you ever tried plugging something in blind? If so, you already know the problem firsthand. If not, go try it now. I'll wait.

The Solution

My design is intentionally lo-fi. A tactile guide added to both the plug and the outlet creates a physical reference point, allowing users to align and insert the plug through touch instead of sight.

Testing Method

For testing, users were blindfolded and asked to use a traditional plug and outlet. Then, still blindfolded, they were given the prototype and asked to talk through their tactile experience.

Participant Response

"So I guess this indented add on is to help me orient the plug without having to touch the metal prongs."

Participant Response

"When I reach out to the outlet, I can find this attachment, which I can feel has an indent on it,"

Participant Response

"and I guess I can put my finger on it, and it helps me, with my knuckle, to center the plug"

Participant Response

"now I can use my thumb and my knuckle to insert the plug into the outlet."

Participant Response

"Wow, that made it easy to know where to put it because you have that alignment between your knuckle and your thumb"
