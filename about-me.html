About Me
Hi, I'm

Amber Grey

I'm a UX researcher and interaction designer interested in what moments of tension reveal about how systems actually work. My work focuses on identifying behavioral patterns within complex experiences and translating those insights into meaningful design interventions. I learn by building.

Approach

Research-driven,
design-led

I'm especially interested in how AI can extend the research process by uncovering patterns, deepening understanding, and transforming insight into experiences that improve clarity, efficiency, and human outcomes.

Behavioral Patterns

Human Outcomes

In My Free Time

I'm a gorilla girl

A unique part of my life is spending time with gorillas. Each week I sit nearby observing, sketching, and simply watching how they interact with each other and with the environment around them.

Spending time in that setting shifts my perspective beyond purely human-centered thinking. It's a reminder that many systems we design exist within much larger ecosystems.

Over the years, these quiet observations have shaped how I think about design. They encouraging me to imagine spaces where animal welfare, curiosity, and learning can exist together.
