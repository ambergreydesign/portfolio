01 — The Brief

What if our world had taken a different path?

MaterGenesis explores an alternate timeline where the patriarchy never came into existence and women were always treated as equals — honored as life givers and spiritual leaders. Born as part of an IADS capstone project, my focus areas were Art, Religion, and Ecology. The project used literature reviews to inform AI-generated prompts, producing imagery that paints a picture of this imagined world.

The broader group project — "A World Without the Patriarchy, According to AI" — brought together four researchers, each exploring what different domains would look like without male-dominated power structures: graphic design, architecture, fashion, colonization, and my own focus on ecology, religion, and art through the MaterGenesis lens.

02 — The Problem

Systems shaped by what was never questioned

Deeply ingrained gender biases, unsustainable environmental practices, and the lingering effects of colonial power structures continue to shape societal dynamics — resulting in economic disparities, ecological degradation, and cultural erasure.

Despite global efforts toward equality and sustainability, progress remains hindered by systemic barriers and entrenched mindsets. MaterGenesis addresses these challenges by constructing a speculative world where those barriers never formed — and then asking what we can learn from the difference.

03 — Core Themes

Three pillars of an alternate foundation

The MaterGenesis universe is built on three interlocking systems that, in our timeline, were disrupted or dismantled. Each became a lens for worldbuilding.

Pillar One
Gender Equity

A world where matriarchal structures evolved alongside egalitarian governance — where power was distributed, not concentrated. Communities organized around balance rather than dominance.

Pillar Two
Ecological Harmony

Architecture, agriculture, and industry developed in symbiosis with the natural world. Sustainability wasn't a movement — it was the default. Cities grew with forests, not over them.

Pillar Three
Cultural Preservation

Indigenous knowledge systems, languages, and traditions were never disrupted. Cultural exchange happened through dialogue, not extraction. Preservation was woven into governance itself.

04 — Research Methodology

From brainstorming to a visual world

The initial brainstorming phase began with a single question: "How might the world be different?" From there, the exploration moved through defining matriarchy vs. patriarchy, mapping historical and contemporary power structures through block definition diagrams and Venn diagrams, and ultimately arriving at a universal prompt: "What if women weren't economically dependent on men?"

This foundation informed three focal domains — Ecology, Religion, and Art — each explored through a systematic pipeline: literature review, concept listing, ChatGPT prompt refinement, and MidJourney image generation. The results were assembled into the MaterGenesis Concept Map.

Phase 01
Brainstorming

Defined matriarchy vs. patriarchy. Built block diagrams, Venn diagrams, and hierarchical maps. Identified the core "What if?" question.

Phase 02
Literature Review

Deep research into each domain — ecological systems, belief structures, and creative expression — to build a foundation of real-world knowledge.

Phase 03
Concept Map

Built a visual map with "Matriarchal World" at the center, branching into Art, Ecology, and Religion — each with sub-categories and prompt-ready details.

Phase 04
Prompt → Image

Taught ChatGPT to construct MidJourney prompts from the map. Each branch produced a unique prompt, generating four images per concept, assembled in Photoshop.

05 — Three Research Domains

Ecology, Religion, and Art — reimagined

Each domain began with a fundamental question, explored through its own pipeline of research, listing, and AI visualization. Together they form the three branches of the MaterGenesis Concept Map.

Ecology
Would life thrive?

Starting with "Would life thrive in a matriarchal world?", this domain explored how ecological systems might diverge — and which species currently functionally extinct might still flourish. The research produced an extinction timeline and visualizations of species like the northern white rhino, Javan wildlife, and Yangtze River dolphins thriving in untouched habitats.

Religion
Belief without patriarchy

This domain peeled back layers of belief systems to find what would persist without human hierarchy. The research honed in on a belief system linked with nature, where women as stewards of worship sought enlightenment through the natural world. It culminated in the MaterGenesis Creation Story — an origin narrative for this alternate world.

Art
Creativity intertwined with nature

The art domain envisioned a world where creativity seamlessly intertwined with nature. Brainstorming produced a list of creative facets that would take distinct form in this imagined world — from inclusive art institutions and community-based collaboration to gender-equal art criticism and preservation practices.

06 — The Creation Story

An origin narrative for a world that never broke

The Religion domain produced something unexpected — a complete creation myth for the MaterGenesis universe. Beginning with "a vast and tranquil void, a canvas of endless potential," the narrative describes a primordial consciousness known as "The Mother," an embodiment of the essence of creation itself.

This creation story became the anchor for the entire project. It wasn't just a narrative device — it was the lens through which every AI prompt was written. Each MidJourney prompt embedded the world's foundational values: women as equals in leadership, revered as life givers, honored as spiritual wisdom holders. The prompts spanned goddess-centered worship, nature-based rituals, inclusive art institutions, eco-feminist agriculture, and species that never went extinct.

Religion Prompts
Sacred rituals & divine worship

Goddess-centered ceremonies, matriarchal mythology, spiritual guidance by women and men equally, nature-based healing practices, and rituals where roles are determined by ability — not gender.

Art Prompts
Inclusive institutions & community creation

Equitable art museums, interdisciplinary education led by women, balanced art criticism, gender-inclusive preservation, and collaborative community art projects rooted in spiritual practice.

Ecology Prompts
Thriving species & environmental stewardship

Sustainable agriculture, eco-feminist values, renewable energy, sacred groves, and vivid scenes of functionally extinct species — white rhinos, Yangtze dolphins, golden toads — alive in untouched habitats.

07 — Research Signals

What the worldbuilding revealed

Four signals kept surfacing across the research — not as answers, but as lenses that reframed the questions worth asking about our own timeline.

Structural Signal

Power Shapes Everything

Every system in the MaterGenesis world — from agriculture to architecture to governance — reflected the foundational distribution of power. When power was shared, every downstream system was different.

Implication: Design interventions targeting outcomes without addressing power structures will always be surface-level.

Ecological Signal

Sustainability Is a Default, Not a Feature

In this timeline, ecological sustainability was never framed as an aspiration. It was the baseline. When nature isn't treated as an externality, the entire economic and architectural logic shifts.

Implication: Our sustainability frameworks are built on the assumption that destruction is the default. Speculative design can challenge that assumption.

Cultural Signal

Preservation as Governance

Cultural preservation wasn't a program or a policy — it was woven into governance itself. Language, tradition, and knowledge were treated as infrastructure, not heritage.

Implication: Treating culture as something to "preserve" already positions it as separate from progress.

Narrative Signal

Imagination Is a Research Method

The act of building an alternate world wasn't just creative expression — it generated real design insights. Speculative worldbuilding forced decisions that revealed assumptions invisible in our timeline.

Implication: Speculative design isn't escapism. It's a diagnostic tool for the present.

08 — The Group Project

Four lenses on a world without patriarchy

MaterGenesis was my contribution to a larger IADS capstone collaboration. Each team member used the same AI-driven methodology — literature review, ChatGPT prompt design, image generation — to explore a different domain of this alternate world. Together, the work formed a comprehensive picture of what "according to AI" a world without patriarchy might look like.

The project culminated in a presentation and interactive worksheet activity where participants crafted their own MidJourney prompts from the creation story — turning the audience from spectators into co-creators of this speculative world.

09 — What It Became

A mirror built from imagination

What started as a capstone "What if?" became a comprehensive speculative universe — a concept map with three branching worlds, a creation myth, dozens of AI-generated scenes, an extinction timeline of species that might have survived, and a group collaboration that asked AI itself to picture a world without patriarchy.

The project's most lasting insight wasn't any single image or prompt. It was the discovery that AI — trained on our world's data — still carries the biases of this timeline. The iterative work of minimizing that bias, of teaching a machine to imagine equity, became its own form of research. MaterGenesis doesn't just show an alternate world. It reveals how deeply this one is encoded into everything we build — including our tools.
