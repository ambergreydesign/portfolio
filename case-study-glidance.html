01 — The Brief

A tool company without a map of who needs it.

Glidance makes mobility aids for blind and low-vision individuals. But like most assistive technology companies, their outreach centered on the most visible users — recently diagnosed, tech-ready, independently mobile.

Our team of international, intercultural designers was brought in to expand that lens. Across 15 interviews with BLV individuals and caregivers, we surfaced 8 systemic barriers preventing people from finding, trusting, or adopting Glidance's products. The challenge then became: how do you build 8 bridges — strategies that actually meet people inside the real complexity of their lives?

The result: 8 strategies, 11 product and outreach concepts, and an interactive mapping tool to visualize the support ecosystem surrounding every BLV individual — the latter researched and built by me.

02 — Actual Lived Experiences

Four people the system wasn't built around.

From 15 interviews, we developed four composite personas — not archetypes, but anchored in specific lived experiences. Each represents a distinct relationship with vision loss, independence, and the support structures that surround them.

Phase 4 · Depression · Risk
Howard
56 — Diabetic Retinopathy
  • Alone — disconnected from family and community
  • Bored — trapped in a care home, unable to go outside
  • Scared — lack of trust in the world, fears independence
Goal: Restore Howard's independence.
Phase 5 · Reaffirmation · Relationship
Lisa
40+ — Hemianopia (50% bilateral vision loss)
  • Relies entirely on her family to get around
  • Misses being active and independent
  • Wants to reduce confusion with others in public
Goal: Help Lisa reclaim active independence.
Phase 3 · Withdrawal · Relationship
Joon
Elder — gradual vision loss
  • Treated like a child by his own family
  • Family unaware that other support options exist
  • Lost dignity and authority within his household
Goal: Empower Joon's family to support him differently.
Phase 7 · Self-Acceptance · Stigma
Khan
Acid attack survivor — partial vision loss
  • Vision loss from trauma, not age or progressive condition
  • Positive outlook constrained by public perception
  • Wants to travel safely without feeling marked
Goal: Enable Khan to move through the world on his terms.

06 — Concepts

Three horizons, eleven concepts.

The 8 strategies informed three time horizons for Glidance — from awareness-building in the near term, through adoption in the mid term, to aspirational product innovation long term. Crawl, Walk, Run.

Horizon 1 · Crawl
Awareness
0–3 years
Spread awareness of Glidance to different communities of BLV people and caregivers — building reach before asking for adoption.
  • 1.1Provider Partnerships
  • 1.2Vision Loss Campaign
  • 1.3Mentor Program
Horizon 2 · Walk
Adoption
3–5 years
Increase adoption of Glidance products and programs through deeper integration into BLV individuals' daily lives and support systems.
  • 2.1Onboarding
  • 2.2Caretaker Check-In
  • 2.3Celebrating Small Wins
  • 2.4Confidence Climbing
  • 2.5Customizable Grip
Horizon 3 · Run
Aspiration
5+ years
Create aspiration by addressing needs that extend beyond Glidance's current product line — positioning the brand as a long-term life partner.
  • 3.1Hands Free
  • 3.2Fun Ways to Learn
  • 3.3Smart Shirt
